# Fab loss profiles and compatible-run attribution

Status: V6 project-aware measured loss ranking retains exact event-backed Q-time step contributors, measures productive-equipment input starvation only inside observed inter-job opportunity windows, partitions local transport blocking by immediate physical cause, traces verified yield outcomes back to observed applied defect-origin events, reports mode-level authored/prevented/applied quality evidence, includes value-weighted delivery mismatch, and is projected from both hash-compatible runs and loss-guided Design iterations.

Related: [[docs/design/industrial-boundaries]], [[docs/design/lot-tracking]], [[docs/design/product-routes]], [[docs/design/operator-workbench]], [[docs/design/design-programs]], [[docs/CLI]], [[plans/memory-fab-design-loop]], [[plans/loss-guided-design]].

## Purpose and authority

Nominal production analysis answers whether configured jobs and material contracts can balance. It does not explain why a re-entrant fab lost realized service during one operating window. Once a completed run exactly matches the current engine, catalogs, World, Blueprint, Scenario, Objective, and selection, the workbench may derive a second evidence layer from its recorded `FactoryMetrics` and `FactoryEvent` stream.

`analyzeFabLossProfile()` is a deterministic source-neutral projection of existing evaluator-owned measurements and events plus the compiled project's Device capabilities. It does not simulate again, change an evaluator score, or claim calibrated semiconductor economics. Every normalization, including active-device utilization weighting, is returned as scalar evidence. `analyzeFabLosses()` adds one hash-compatible persisted-run identity to that profile for Workbench use; the ranking model itself owns no run provenance.

## Compatibility gate

A run is current only when World, Blueprint, Scenario, and Objective ids plus the engine, catalogs, World, Blueprint, Scenario, and Objective hashes all match the compiled project. Selection equality or engine equality alone is insufficient. An older run remains historical evidence but cannot drive current loss attribution or outrank current nominal diagnostics.

## Named buckets

For a run with a tracked lot family, Core ranks non-zero measured signals in these stable buckets:

- delivery portfolio: mean unmet share across evaluator-owned contracts, with exact demanded, delivered, shortfall, above-demand output, gross value, shortage penalty, and net value evidence;
- release and admission: pending scheduled lots plus CONWIP/control and physical-capacity release blocks;
- tracked-lot queue congestion: mean lot queue time as a share of mean cycle time, with process/move time and the measured productive bottleneck context retained separately;
- productive-equipment input starvation: gaps between a productive Device's completed job and its next productive start, excluding overlapping maintenance/qualification, changeover, failure, output blocking, batch/campaign hold, tooling/facility wait, sleep/wake, and power intervals. The opportunity denominator spans first observed start through last observed finish, while warm-up before the first start, drain after the last finish, single-job Devices, and exception-only rework equipment remain auditable boundary evidence rather than ranked starvation;
- batch formation: formation holds, timeout releases, and per-lot batch wait;
- setup and campaign control: powered changeover work and campaign holds;
- maintenance and qualification: service, qualification, consumable wait, crew wait, and cancellation evidence;
- reusable tooling contention and fab facility contention: their independent provider waits, blocks, cancellations, and interruptions;
- equipment failure and power interruption: measured Device status time;
- local transport blocking by cause: only measured blocked item-time is ranked. Every blocked transit carries one immediate physical cause and stage: line-cell contention, endpoint service capacity, endpoint power interruption, or endpoint failure at the line, loader, or unloader. Tracked-lot mean transport time remains explicit cycle-time and bucket context, but ordinary required movement contributes no loss score by itself;
- Route Q-time: violated lots and step visits, with ordered event-backed contributors grouped by Route step and waiting mechanism. Every contributor retains Process ids, Route and physical Device subjects, unique lot and visit counts, total/mean/maximum queue time, the evaluator-owned limit, and total/maximum overrun. A multi-lot Process with no overlapping service work is classified as `batch-companion-wait`; service or qualification events overlapping the violating lot's queue interval are classified as `maintenance-qualification` and retain their provider Device; the honest fallback is `equipment-availability`;
- verified yield and quality loss: first-pass results over actually inspected unique lots, plus rework, scrap, escapes, and lost lot-derived output; unfinished WIP is not called a quality failure. Ordered contributors group authored quality excursions, equipment process drift, and defect-bearing Route Q-time violations by exact source Process, Device, and Route step, then follow each source lot/defect pair through detection, rework attempt, repair, persistence, quality scrap, or output-profile escape. Equipment-drift aggregates remain available as independent cross-check evidence.

Each bucket retains exact scalar evidence, a contributor array, and the strongest available Device, connection, Route, or project subject. Most buckets expose an empty contributor array rather than inventing detail. Q-time emits Route-step contributors; input starvation emits ordered Device contributors with Process ids, job counts, opportunity span, inter-job gap, excluded unavailability, remaining starvation, utilization, raw input wait, and boundary wait. Transport blocking emits only positive connection contributors with exact Resource mix, departed/delivered flow, capacity, utilization, average in-flight inventory, total blocked item-ticks, blocked fraction, the complete four-cause partition, and endpoint-stage capacity/power/failure shares. The contributor mechanism is its largest immediate cause, while all shares remain visible; a queue propagated into an occupied upstream cell remains line contention at that transit rather than being rewritten as an unproven graph-level root cause. The four cause totals must sum exactly to total blocked item-time or attribution rejects the metrics. Contributors sort by blocked time, blocked fraction, then stable connection id. Yield emits defect-origin contributors with exact defect classes and lot ids plus introduced, detected, reworked, repaired, persistent, scrapped, and escaped counts. Yield contributors sort by observed scrap, escape, persistence, rework, introduced lots, then stable id; because a set-valued latent defect may receive multiple observed introductions, contributors are investigative chains rather than an additive decomposition. Input contributors sort by `starvationTicks × utilization`, then starvation and stable id; utilization selects an investigation subject but does not change the bucket denominator. Queue congestion still prefers the evaluator's productive bottleneck. Zero-signal buckets are omitted. Results are deterministically sorted by descending score and stable id; Q-time contributors sort by visit count, total overrun, then stable id. `primary` is the first bucket and `chain` is the first five bucket ids. Delivery overflow is reported as context but is not itself a loss because above-demand product remains valuable under [[docs/design/delivery-contracts]].

## Interpretation boundary

Bucket scores normalize heterogeneous measured delays and counts so an operator or Agent can prioritize investigation. Several signals overlap—for example, a batch hold contributes to queue time, and a power interruption can extend Route Q-time. Scores are therefore not additive units of foregone output, a Shapley decomposition, or proof that changing the top bucket will recover a stated quantity.

The contract always carries this caveat. Product language uses “ranked signal” and “loss chain,” not fabricated percentage causality. A future counterfactual attribution model must be a separate evaluator contract with controlled reruns.

## Workbench projection

`ProjectWorkbenchSnapshot` version 6 includes `lossAttribution` or `null`. Compatible-run diagnostics use `fab-loss.<bucket>` codes, priorities 90 down to 86 for the top five, and `compatible-run` evidence with the exact run id. Capacity blockers remain priority 100. This makes realized fab evidence outrank generic nominal warnings only after the compatibility gate passes.

`inm inspect --section losses --json` returns the complete structured attribution. The default machine summary includes the outcome, complete primary bucket, chain, and caveat. Human `inm inspect` prints measured quality-origin, input-gap, transport-blocking, and Q-time contributor rows even after another signal becomes primary. Studio uses the same object for the Realized Fab Loss Chain panel and renders matching defect/lot outcome chains, Device/process opportunity gaps, blocked-connection flow evidence, and Q-time step/mechanism evidence without parsing prose or inspecting raw NDJSON. When compatible loss targets an aligned Design Program, Workbench V6 first checks current-input Design evidence: no evidence opens the Program brief, while promotable, continuable, or exhausted evidence reopens the exact immutable run.

Design Run `c0d09455644c712e60daa78dceded85e39cd7587d3c09019003558497499a56a` proves why the Blueprint change is nevertheless real: changing only `lithography-l2` lot dispatch to earliest due date preserves four locked current-best cases and improves facility interruption by `+0.053333`, while wider EDD variants regress the lithography-interruption case. ## Design iteration projection

A Design Program executes the exact driver case against its current best Blueprint before every proposal. Core hashes those complete driver metrics and derives a source-neutral `FabLossProfile` from them. The immutable iteration records both as `driverEvidence`; it never invents a persisted run id or calls invocation-local evidence “compatible.”

Project proposal-provider API V6 receives a deeply frozen copy of the same V6 profile, explicit selected-branch identity, proposal-time leader comparison, and lineage-local history. A provider therefore sees whether measured transport blocking is dominated by line contention, endpoint service, endpoint power, or endpoint failure before it names an intervention; endpoint or power evidence is not evidence for increasing belt speed. For a promotion-ready leader whose ranked `chain` is non-empty, the provider must return `addressedLoss` naming one bucket in that chain. For an alternative with guardrail violations, it instead must return `addressedCase` naming one current promotion blocker and may omit `addressedLoss`; facility resilience must not be mislabeled as a mixed-quality loss intervention. Missing and fabricated targets are rejected before candidate compilation. Historical targets describe attempts made from this Blueprint lineage, including rejected ones; they do not alter the current chain or promotion boundary. Either target is a falsifiable rationale, not proof that its patch will succeed: the complete locked Benchmark and frontier policy still supply KEEP/BRANCH/REJECT authority.

The memory-fab provider annotates ordinary candidates with loss buckets and repair candidates with locked case ids. It chooses a case repair first when the selected alternative is blocked; otherwise it ranks the least-attempted eligible loss before chain position and authored order. It never repeats a strategy, skips a patch whose exact policy is already installed, and stops when it has no matching intervention. One repair adds ordinary powered N+1 fab-utility capacity for `facility-interruption`; a later repair adds spatially independent N+2 capacity only after a dedicated layer-two lithography bay exists. Another recognizes only the exact existing `3 / 12000` lithography campaign and removes voluntary hold for `lithography-interruption`.

On the exact initially commissioned factory, the preceding profile ranked `delivery-portfolio` first because 48 commercial devices included 16 above demand while performance was 12 short and automotive was 6 short, leaving `-48` net value. The bounded `dispatch:burn-in-contract-value` proposal changed only the existing rack policy. V3 then separated burn-in input starvation, lithography-context queue congestion, batch wait, Q-time, and the remaining commercial contract gap; verified first-pass yield was `5/6`, not the old `6/12` completion-derived “good yield.” That evidence led to a physically routed dedicated layer-two lithography bay. Its first evaluation became a branch because one surviving utility plant could not support the expanded vacuum/exhaust load during `facility-interruption`; N+2 utility repair made every locked case improve.

Historical compatible run `057-simulate` delivered 40 devices and left 10 commercial units short. Mean queue time fell from 48.9 to 39.2 seconds and etch became the bottleneck context. More WIP reached inspection, exposing the opportunity-window-aware chain `yield-quality → q-time → queue-congestion → input-starvation → batch-formation`: 5 of 12 inspected lots passed first inspection, 6 reworked, and 2 scrapped. `etch-1` was the leading physical quality subject because it introduced 6 of the run's 8 recorded equipment-drift defect instances.

Project providers may attach exact Device subjects to intervention families. Within the same loss rank and attempt count, a proposal matching the observed subject is tried before a generic or unrelated intervention. Subject matching selected an explicit six-job etch preventive-maintenance experiment first, but the five-case Benchmark rejected it: drift defects fell `8 → 5` while completed lots fell `6 → 5`, so the physical maintenance cost outweighed the isolated quality gain. A bounded project-local TypeScript sweep then identified a system intervention rather than weakening that cost: split layer-two etch onto a routed physical bay, service both etch bays before five jobs, and select deep final-pattern inspection to catch the latent-electrical defects that otherwise escaped when added capacity brought more lots to completion. Design Run `effced617779ef8988476294a349f80aec3a79c9824cd052b8fc75b9d4912e16` improved all five current-best cases and promoted the exact 27-operation Candidate.

Compatible run `058-simulate` is the Q-time intervention's immutable before state. It delivers 45 devices, preserves performance `12/12` and automotive `6/6`, reduces commercial shortfall `10 → 5`, raises portfolio net value `+144 → +164`, reduces rework cycles `6 → 5`, scrap `2 → 1`, and equipment-drift defects `8 → 2`, with no quality escape. The leading quality subject moves from etch to `lithography-1`; both etch bays record zero drift defects. Because the quality intervention removed the preceding top signal instead of freezing the old diagnosis, its chain becomes `q-time → input-starvation → yield-quality → queue-congestion → maintenance-qualification`.

The V4 Q-time evidence separates that aggregate into two physical contributors. `anneal-dielectric-stack` records three lots and three visits on `furnace-1`, `103.6` total queue seconds against a `20`-second limit, and `43.6` total overrun seconds; its three-lot Process identifies batch-companion wait. `final-inspection` records three lots and three visits on `inspection-1`, `120.9` total queue seconds against a `35`-second limit, and `15.9` total overrun seconds; overlapping mandatory service and qualification events identify maintenance/qualification wait and retain `maintenance-service-1` as the provider subject. Both CLI and Studio consume these same contributors.

Current compatible run `059-simulate` is the reviewed Candidate's after state. The Blueprint keeps both fixed three-lot and rapid one-lot anneal Processes, prefers the batch only when it is already ready, sets its formation wait to zero, and replaces the single-crew service bay with a project-local two-crew asset. Anneal maximum queue falls `40.8 → 0.9` seconds and its three batch-companion violations disappear. Total Q-time falls from six visits across five lots to two visits across two lots. Those remaining visits are both final-inspection maintenance/qualification waits; because the improved factory brings every lot to a terminal disposition and performs sixteen inspection visits, their accumulated overrun rises to `83.6` seconds rather than being hidden behind unfinished WIP.

The same run completes `8/12` lots with four explicit scrap dispositions and no in-progress lots, versus `6` completed, one scrapped, and five unfinished before. It raises first-pass completions `5 → 8`, first-pass yield `0.417 → 0.667`, lowers rework `5 → 4`, preserves two drift defects and zero escapes, fulfills all three contracts with commercial `38/32`, performance `12/12`, automotive `6/6`, and raises portfolio net value `+164 → +196`. The next measured primary signal is therefore verified yield and quality, not the now-reduced Q-time aggregate.

Current compatible run `065-simulate` shows why input waiting requires a bounded opportunity window. Its finite twelve-lot campaign releases through tick `66,000` and reaches twelve terminal dispositions inside a `240,000`-tick Scenario. Raw active productive-equipment input wait is `1,617,000` ticks, but `1,220,000` of `1,401,000` flow-equipment ticks are outside ranked inter-job gaps and another `216,000` belong to exception-only equipment. After excluding `94,000` ticks of event-backed unavailability, eight of ten active flow Devices retain `181,000` starvation ticks inside `1,133,000` opportunity ticks. `probe-1`, not packaging boundary idle, is the leading contributor with `50,000` starvation ticks across nine jobs and `122,000` opportunity ticks. The corrected chain begins `yield-quality → queue-congestion → q-time → input-starvation → maintenance-qualification`; the shared next action therefore investigates verified yield instead of proposing capacity against normal warm-up and campaign drain.

That same run's verified-yield aggregate now resolves into two observed origin chains. Three authored `etch-cell-layer-2` excursions on `etch-l2` introduce critical-dimension, particle-contamination, and latent-electrical defects. All three are detected and reworked; critical dimension is repaired, while the other two persist and end in quality scrap. Lot `dram-lot-03` would otherwise recover, but its second final inspection waits `80.8` seconds against a `35`-second Q-time limit; the violation introduces a new particle-contamination defect that is detected and scrapped at that inspection. The bucket therefore names `etch-l2` as its leading physical subject while retaining final-inspection Q-time as a separate one-lot contributor. Quality-stress run `067-simulate` independently scales the same contract to six authored excursion lots with four persistent scraps and four Q-time-origin scraps; contributor overlap remains visible rather than falsely summed.

Historical compatible run `068-simulate` followed commissioning of the then-selected `9/6 EDD` release controller. All twelve lots still reached terminal disposition and all three product contracts remained fulfilled, but the different replenishment sequence produced eight first-pass completions, four reworks, and four scraps. The same two source mechanisms remained explicit: three `etch-l2` authored excursions led to two persistent scraps, while two final-inspection Q-time defects led to two scrap dispositions. Mean queue improved to `15.57` seconds and the mixed-quality case score rose `+0.417884`, so the measured chain became `yield-quality → q-time → queue-congestion → input-starvation → maintenance-qualification`. Later identity-safe release research superseded that controller; the run remains evidence for its historical regime, not current project guidance.

Historical compatible run `070-simulate` followed reviewed Candidate `continuous-deep-metrology`. Replacing only the inspection asset without retuning admission regressed the steady current-best score, so the accepted Design proposal coupled its longer qualification interval and higher inspection capacity to a `7/4 EDD` release window. The mixed case improved to ten completed lots, nine first-pass lots, three reworks, two scraps, zero escapes, zero pending releases, and zero Q-time violations. The only quality-origin contributor was the fixed `etch-cell-layer-2` excursion: three lots were detected and reworked, one repaired, and two persisted to scrap. Its Blueprint, event stream, metrics, and final state were byte-identical to `069-simulate`; the new Run only restored evidence compatibility after the optional Process, Device, and Route catalogs were expanded. Its V4 measured chain was `yield-quality → input-starvation → queue-congestion → transport-blocking → maintenance-qualification`.

The resulting intervention boundary is now testable rather than hypothetical. Advanced pattern recovery repairs the particle-contamination branch and improves four of five locked cases, but Design records a `0.429259` current-best regression under timed lithography interruption. The branch remains non-dominated evidence without becoming the selected Blueprint. Fab-loss attribution must therefore continue to describe the two scraps of the commissioned Process, while Catalog and Design surfaces separately expose the available technology and its blocker; mixing those states would falsely report a laboratory option as installed equipment.

Mode-level prevention keeps that boundary causal. `analyzeQualityContributors()` counts only residual `event.defects`, while the yield bucket separately reports how many fixed `authoredDefects` were prevented before latent lot state changed. A fully prevented excursion therefore remains auditable without becoming a false loss contributor. Candidate `closed-loop-layer-two-etch` uses this distinction to prevent only latent electrical damage at the existing layer-two Process; critical-dimension and particle-contamination origins remain attributable to their real equipment and Route subjects.

Compatible run `074-simulate` proved the V4 quality projection after guarded commissioning. It reports three authored, one prevented, and two applied fixed defect instances; ten of twelve lots pass first inspection, the two residual applied defects are repaired, and no lot scraps or escapes. The yield contributor therefore retains only `dram-lot-03` and `dram-lot-08`, while the separate prevention aggregate retains `dram-lot-11`, `etch-l2`, and `closed-loop-control`. Its then-current V4 chain included `transport-blocking` because ordinary transit was still ranked; V5 corrects that attribution without changing the immutable run's physical events or metrics.

Subject matching remains proposal priority, not acceptance authority. The locked Benchmark decides whether a child becomes leader, and a reviewed Candidate remains the only route to the live Blueprint. The provider falls back to authored order only when there is no tracked-route profile. CLI progress and immutable JSON, plus Studio live and reopened result views, project the same observed chain, proposal boundary, and selected target history.
